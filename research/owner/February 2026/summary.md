# Owner Research Summary — February 2026

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from business owner interviews and persona analysis conducted in February 2026. The research focuses on transportation company owners managing small to medium-sized fleets (0-30 trucks), examining their business goals, operational challenges, and decision-making criteria for fleet management tools and investments.

## Key Findings

### Core Role & Environment

Business owners operate at the intersection of operations, finance, and strategy. Their role and challenges vary significantly by fleet size, from extreme multitasking in very small companies to process-driven management in medium-sized operations.

### Primary Goals

Owners' goals evolve with company size but consistently focus on **financial stability, operational predictability, cost transparency, and reducing manual workload** to protect margins and enable growth.

### Critical Pain Points

1. **Financial Pressure**: Slim margins, liquidity problems, and high non-payment risk create constant financial stress, especially in very small companies.

2. **Tool Fragmentation**: Fragmented tools (WhatsApp + portals + paperwork) lead to heavy manual workload, errors, delays, and ultimately financial losses.

3. **Manual Operations**: Limited ability to learn new tools due to time constraints, reliance on phone-based support, and preference for simple, reliable tools.

4. **Regulatory Burden**: Must track changing EU/local rules, creating compliance risk and operational overhead.

5. **Lack of Transparency**: Difficulty gaining visibility into costs, documents, and performance without consolidated views and automation.

### Key Differences by Company Size

**Very Small Company Owner (0-3 trucks):**
- **Goal**: Keep the business alive, protect margins, reduce stress, gain more time for family
- **Extreme multitasking**: Switches constantly between driving, dispatching, customer calls, and paperwork
- **High financial pressure**: Slim margins, liquidity problems, high non-payment risk
- **Highly operational**: Few or no staff; owner does everything manually
- **Tool-conservative**: Wants simple, reliable tools with minimal time investment

**Small Company Owner (4-10 trucks):**
- **Goal**: Centralize operations, reduce manual steps, gain transparency on costs, stabilize cashflow
- **Hands-on operator**: Still personally involved in dispatching and driver coordination
- **Financially cautious**: Monitors prices, limits, and transactions to avoid blocks
- **Time-poor**: Limited ability to learn new tools; relies on phone-based support
- **High regulatory burden**: Must track changing EU/local rules
- **Fragmented tools**: Uses WhatsApp + portals + paperwork, leading to heavy manual workload

**Medium Company Owner (11-30 trucks):**
- **Goal**: Achieve predictable operations, transparent costs, fast payments, high service quality through better tooling and automation
- **Process-driven and relationship-oriented**: Balances daily operations with long-term strategy
- **Partnership-focused**: Expects stability, transparency, and reliability from vendors
- **Data-aware**: Needs consolidated views of costs, documents, and performance
- **Burdened by rising costs and regulatory uncertainty**: Must predict risks and optimize spend
- **Works with more structure**: But still faces communication chaos, scattered data, and delays

### What Owners Want

- **Financial stability** — predictable costs, cash flow, and margins
- **Full cost transparency** — visibility into all expenses and profitability
- **Tight end-to-end processes** — automation and integration
- **Centralized operations** — reduce manual steps and fragmentation
- **Simple, reliable tools** — minimal time investment and learning curve
- **Stability and reliability** — from vendors and systems
- **Predictable operations** — reduce surprises and optimize spend

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Owners value **centralized management, cost transparency, and operational efficiency**. A fleet licensing solution that enables centralized purchase and management of navigation licenses aligns with their goals of reducing fragmentation and gaining control. However, the solution must demonstrate clear ROI, integrate seamlessly into existing workflows, and require minimal learning curve. Owners are financially cautious and need to see value before investing.
