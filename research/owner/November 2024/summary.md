# Owner Research Summary — November 2024

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from a quantitative survey study conducted in November 2024, focusing on mobile track and trace capabilities from the perspective of transportation company owners. The study surveyed 168 owners and decision-makers across various fleet sizes (primarily 2-10 trucks) to understand their technology management practices, communication patterns, and attitudes toward driver tracking and navigation features.

## Key Findings

### Participant Demographics

- **Sample Size**: 168 respondents
- **Primary Role**: 119 are business owners (71% of respondents)
- **Company Types**: 139 work in transportation companies, 25 in freight forwarding
- **Fleet Sizes**: 94 companies operate 2-10 trucks over 3.5 tons (most common), 15 operate 11-20 trucks
- **Geographic Coverage**: Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Portugal, Romania, Spain, Poland

### Technology Management for Drivers

**Mobile Phone Provision:**
- 86 companies (51%) have drivers use private phones for work
- 82 companies (49%) provide company-issued phones
- **Geographic Insight**: Czech Republic (31/86) and Hungary (16/86) most commonly use private phones

**Internet/Roaming Packages:**
- 57 companies offer data packages for both private and business use
- 54 companies offer data packages exclusively for business use
- 53 companies (32%) do NOT offer any internet packages
- 33 companies provide more than 5,000 MB of internet access
- **Geographic Insight**: Czech Republic (40/111), Romania (25/111), Portugal (15/111) more often provide packages; Hungary (12/53) frequently lacks roaming packages

### Communication with Drivers

**Primary Communication Methods:**
- Phone calls: 130 companies (most common, 77%)
- Instant messaging (WhatsApp, Telegram): 92 companies (55%)
- SMS: 66 companies (39%)
- Email: 26 companies (15%)

**Geographic Patterns:**
- **Phone Call Dominance**: Czech Republic (54/130), Slovakia (15/130), Hungary (15/130), Portugal (12/130)
- **Instant Messaging Trend**: Romania (24/92) and Spain (25/92) show greater openness to digital communication

**Advanced Tools:**
- Some companies use specialized fleet management tools: Transics, Dynafleet, Arobs, Volvo Connect, Frotcom
- Indicates need for more advanced fleet monitoring and management solutions

### Fuel Price Visibility Feature

**Attitudes Toward Showing Fuel Prices in Navigation App:**
- 44 owners (26%) think "That would be great!"
- 43 owners (26%) have no opinion (don't use Eurowag Navigation App)
- 23 owners (14%) find it unnecessary because owners/dispatchers plan refueling in advance
- 6 owners (4%) don't like it due to distraction concerns

**Geographic Insight**: Czech Republic (17/44) and Romania (11/44) show strongest positive sentiment

**Key Concerns:**
- Driver distraction from fuel price display
- Pre-planning makes real-time prices less useful
- Suggestion: Show only cheapest stations, target owners/dispatchers rather than drivers

### Location Tracking Attitudes

**Current Usage:**
- 41 companies (27%) already use similar tracking solutions
- 20 companies (13%) view driver tracking as useful

**Attitudes Toward Mobile Phone-Based Driver Tracking:**
- 41 companies (27%): "It's a good idea, but I already use something similar"
- 28 companies (18%): "I like it, but track only during work-related activities"
- 26 companies (17%): "I like it, but afraid drivers don't want to be tracked"
- 24 companies (16%): "I don't like the idea"
- 20 companies (13%): "It's a good idea, I want it"
- 11 companies (7%): Concerned about driver internet data usage

**Geographic Comfort Levels**: Czech Republic (50/126), Hungary (23/126), Spain (6/126), Poland (5/126) show greater comfort with work-hours-only tracking

**Critical Privacy Concerns:**
- Significant concerns about driver privacy
- Many companies indicate drivers may resist tracking outside working hours
- Full tracking perceived as too invasive
- Distinction between vehicle tracking (acceptable) vs. driver tracking (privacy concerns)

### Key Conclusions & Recommendations

**1. Vehicle Tracking vs. Driver Tracking:**
- Companies prefer tracking vehicles rather than drivers directly
- Telematics based on vehicle tracking, not full driver supervision
- Tachograph or installed GPS alone often sufficient for vehicle location
- TrackAndTrace concept (driver sharing location via mobile app) not well understood
- Owners value vehicle/goods tracking, not driver tracking per se

**2. Fuel Price Display:**
- Useful feature but needs careful implementation
- Target owners/dispatchers, not drivers (to avoid distraction)
- Show stations with optimal prices
- Incorporate filters and ability to schedule refueling

**3. Product Recommendations:**
- Telematics products should be scalable and modular for different fleet sizes
- Offer varied feature packages for small vs. large companies
- Educate owners that driver location sharing benefits companies without telematics
- Consider rethinking driver-sharing concept due to privacy concerns

**4. Additional Ideas:**
- Integrated application with GPS, payment solutions (MB WAY), company-linked cards with limits
- Digital TACHOGRAPH and Driver's Card management
- Automated fine insurance contact and legal support
- Fleet support and unlocking services

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Owners value **vehicle-centric solutions** and **centralized management**. They prefer tracking vehicles rather than drivers, indicating a focus on operational visibility rather than personal monitoring. A fleet licensing solution that:
- Provides vehicle-level visibility and control
- Integrates with existing telematics/GPS systems
- Offers modular, scalable packages for different fleet sizes
- Respects driver privacy boundaries
- Targets owners/dispatchers for management features

...would align with owner needs. However, any solution must address privacy concerns and clearly communicate value to companies without existing telematics.
