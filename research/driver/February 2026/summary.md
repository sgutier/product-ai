# Driver Research Summary — February 2026

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from driver interviews and persona analysis conducted in February 2026. The research focuses on professional truck drivers working in small to medium-sized transportation companies (0-50 trucks), examining their needs, workflows, and pain points related to navigation and fleet operations.

## Key Findings

### Core Role & Environment

Drivers operate in a **high-pressure, time-sensitive environment** where they must navigate efficiently, stay compliant, and complete trips on time. Their primary focus is on the road, requiring tools that minimize distraction and provide clear, reliable guidance.

### Primary Goals

Drivers' goals vary by company size but center on **avoiding problems, staying compliant, reducing communication overhead during driving, and operating efficiently with minimal paperwork friction**.

### Critical Pain Points

1. **Navigation Dependency & Anxiety**: Heavy dependency on navigation where unclear routing causes anxiety and "rerouting chaos." Drivers need accurate maps, clear restrictions, and reliable routing.

2. **Time Pressure**: High time pressure from traffic, delays, and unpredictable loading/unloading creates stress and impacts trip completion.

3. **Infrastructure Challenges**: Limited infrastructure support, especially finding safe parking after hours, creates operational friction.

4. **Tool Reliability**: App errors, freezes, or outdated data break trust. Reliability is critical — drivers express frustration when tools fail during critical moments.

5. **Micromanagement**: Drivers value autonomy and prefer to make routing/stop decisions without excessive oversight.

### Key Differences by Company Size

**Small Company Drivers (0-10 trucks):**
- Simple, minimalistic workflow — want few steps, focus entirely on the road
- Goal: Avoid problems, stay compliant, reduce communication overhead during drive
- High time pressure from traffic, delays, unpredictable loading/unloading
- Heavy dependency on navigation — unclear routing causes anxiety and rerouting chaos
- Limited infrastructure support — finding safe parking, especially after hours

**Medium/Large Company Drivers (11-50 trucks):**
- Digitally advanced professionals comfortable with telematics, scanning, and routing apps
- Goal: Drive efficiently with minimal paperwork friction, supported by reliable digital tools
- Value autonomy — prefer making routing/stop decisions without micromanagement
- Operate in complex digital environment requiring accurate maps, clear restrictions, fast UI
- Need predictable costs and infrastructure information (refueling rules, card acceptance, amenities)
- Frustrated by app errors, freezes, outdated data — reliability is critical for trust

### What Drivers Want

- **Short processes** — minimal steps, focus on driving
- **No surprises on the way** — predictable routing and clear restrictions
- **Safe parking** — especially after hours
- **No micromanagement** — autonomy in routing and stop decisions
- **Reliable digital tools** — accurate maps, clear restrictions, fast UI
- **Predictable costs** — refueling rules, card acceptance, amenity information
- **Fast, error-free apps** — no freezes, no outdated data

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Drivers value **reliability and simplicity**. They need navigation tools that "just work" without distraction. A fleet licensing solution that provides premium navigation features (offline maps, traffic, Android/CarPlay) would address core needs, but only if it's reliable and doesn't add complexity. Drivers may not directly purchase licenses, but they benefit from company-provided premium features that improve their daily experience.
