# Driver Research Summary — November 2024

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from a quantitative survey study conducted in November 2024, focusing on mobile track and trace capabilities with professional truck drivers. The study surveyed 73 drivers across various company sizes (from 1 truck to 51+ trucks) to understand their technology usage, communication patterns, and attitudes toward location sharing and navigation features.

## Key Findings

### Participant Demographics

- **Sample Size**: 73 drivers
- **Company Types**: 63 work in transport, 13 in freight forwarding, 4 in production/other with own transport
- **Fleet Sizes**: 23 operate 2-10 trucks, 15 operate 51+ trucks, 13 operate 11-20 trucks
- **Main Contact**: 41 consider dispatchers as main contact, 26 consider owners
- **Communication Methods**: Phone calls (49), WhatsApp/communicators (41), SMS (19)

### Technology Usage Patterns

**Mobile Phone Usage:**
- 55 drivers (75%) use private mobile phones for work
- 15 drivers (21%) have company-provided phones
- High reliance on personal devices for work tasks

**Daily Work Tools:**
- Navigation in mobile phone: 55 drivers (most common)
- Tachographs: 51 drivers
- Communicators (WhatsApp, Viber, etc.): 47 drivers
- Tablets: 26 drivers
- External navigation: 21 drivers
- Laptops: 14 drivers
- Integrated navigation: 13 drivers
- Telematics: 7 drivers

### Data and Connectivity

**Roaming Data Packages:**
- 49 drivers (67%) do NOT receive company-provided international roaming data packages
- 12 receive roaming for both private and business use
- 12 receive roaming only for business use
- Mixed company policies create inconsistency

**Monthly Data Limits:**
- 25 drivers (41%) have unlimited data
- 14 drivers have 10,001 MB or more
- Most drivers have ample data for work needs

### Location Sharing Attitudes

**Experience with Location Sharing:**
- 58 drivers (79%) have experience sharing location via mobile apps
- 15 drivers have no experience

**Attitudes Toward Real-Time Tracking:**
- 20 drivers (29%) think it's a "good idea"
- 17 drivers (25%) like it but prefer tracking only for work-related activities
- 16 drivers (24%) like it but already use similar systems
- 6 drivers (9%) don't like the idea (privacy concerns)
- 5 drivers (7%) like it but worry about data usage

**Key Privacy Concerns:**
- Clear distinction between private phone vs. company phone
- Preference for work-only tracking, not personal time
- Desire for control over who can see location
- Some already have GPS tracking via vehicle systems

### Fuel Price Visibility Feature

**Attitudes Toward Showing Fuel Prices in Navigation App:**
- 34 drivers (51%) think "That would be great!"
- 15 drivers (22%) find it "not useful" because contact person plans refueling in advance
- 7 drivers (10%) don't like it due to distraction concerns
- 5 drivers don't use the Eurowag app
- 4 drivers already use other apps for fuel prices

### Referral Reward Preferences

When asked what reward would motivate them to refer new navigation software:
- Equipment for more comfortable time in truck cabin: 23 drivers (32%)
- Automotive accessories (premium speed cameras, Android Auto): 17 drivers (23%)
- Shopping vouchers (Allegro, Amazon, etc.): 12 drivers (16%)
- Premium navigation trial license: 12 drivers (16%)
- Restaurant vouchers: 3 drivers
- Netflix/Spotify/Xbox subscriptions: 1 driver

**Insight**: Drivers prefer practical rewards that enhance work experience or offer flexibility.

### Additional Feedback Themes

1. **App Stability**: Reports of crashes, especially in busy areas. Need for better navigation accuracy (tonnage ban zones, ADR routing).

2. **Tracking Preferences**: Skepticism about continuous tracking. Prefer alerts only when vehicle is in danger or facing technical issues.

3. **Navigation Features**: Requests for better route diversion (road closures, low bridges, weight restrictions), accommodation for driver breaks, improved offline navigation.

4. **Missing Features**: Ability to plan routes on PC (from previous version), better search capabilities.

5. **General Improvements**: Better graphics, more reliable GPS, improved customer service.

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Drivers show **high reliance on mobile navigation** (55 out of 73 use navigation in mobile phone daily) and **preference for practical, work-enhancing features**. They value:
- Reliability and stability (critical pain point)
- Work-focused features (not personal tracking)
- Practical rewards and incentives
- Control over privacy and data usage

A fleet licensing solution that provides reliable, work-focused navigation features would align with driver needs, but must address stability concerns and respect privacy boundaries.
