# Driver Research Summary — December 2025

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from persona analysis conducted in December 2025, focusing on owner-driver hybrid personas in small transportation businesses (1-10 trucks) across Poland, Slovakia, and Czech Republic. These personas represent individuals who combine multiple roles (owner, dispatcher, driver) in small family businesses or micro-enterprises.

## Key Findings

### Core Role & Environment

These owner-drivers operate in **highly operational, multitasking environments** where they must balance driving, dispatching, financial management, and business operations. They often work with external freight forwarding companies and rely on a mix of digital and analog tools to manage their businesses.

### Primary Goals

Owner-drivers' primary goals center on **maintaining independence and financial stability, simplifying administration, and growing the business sustainably**. They seek tools that reduce complexity, save time, and provide clear visibility into costs and profitability.

### Critical Pain Points

1. **Financial Instability**: Limited capital reserves, long payment terms, dependency on clients, and fear of non-payment create constant financial stress. Cash flow management is critical.

2. **Tool Fragmentation**: Using multiple disconnected tools (WhatsApp, Excel, freight exchanges, invoicing tools, fuel card panels) creates chaos, errors, and manual workload.

3. **Time Poverty**: Extreme multitasking between driving, dispatching, customer calls, and paperwork leaves little time for learning new tools or business development.

4. **Lack of Integrated Solutions**: No single tool that combines freight exchange, factoring, invoicing, GPS, and fleet management without duplicates or data overload.

5. **Regulatory Burden**: Must track changing EU/local regulations, replace equipment (tachographs, driver cards), and ensure compliance while managing costs.

6. **Operational Disruptions**: Vehicle breakdowns, unexpected route changes, cargo theft, and equipment failures have major impact on small businesses with limited reserves.

### Key Persona Characteristics

**Marek (1 truck, Poland):**
- One-man business, no capital reserves
- Usually drives same routes
- Works with external freight forwarders
- Limited financial capabilities, cash flow similar to employee without benefits

**Tomas (2-5 trucks, Slovakia):**
- "Orchestra man" combining owner/driver/dispatcher roles
- Wife assists with part-time accounting
- Good team of trusted drivers (family/reliable folks)
- Operates in chaos, prioritizes cashflow
- Started as driver in dad's company

**Dawid (6-10 trucks, Poland):**
- Owner/dispatcher/fleet manager
- Manages mixed team (trusted family + newly hired)
- Focuses on full vehicle utilization
- Involved in other businesses (repair, financial sector)
- Open to digitization but needs clear processes

**Average Carlos (1-10 trucks, Slovakia/Czech):**
- Combines owner/dispatcher/driver roles
- May work with external freight forwarding companies
- May have closest as business partners/co-workers
- Visual person, prefers charts over text
- Wants to know what happens with every penny

### What Owner-Drivers Want

- **Everything in one place**: Integrated system (freight exchange, factoring, invoicing, GPS) without duplicates
- **Cost transparency**: Know exactly how much each truck costs, where money is lost
- **Faster payments**: Better debt collection, partners who pay faster
- **Automation**: Reduce manual work, automate repetitive tasks
- **Simple solutions**: Easy, fast, intuitive tools customized to company size
- **Visual data**: Prefer charts and graphics over text-heavy reports
- **Less stress, more family time**: Reduce complications and administrative burden

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Owner-drivers value **centralized management and cost transparency**. They want tools that reduce fragmentation and provide clear visibility into expenses. A fleet licensing solution that integrates seamlessly into their workflow and shows clear cost allocation per truck would address core needs. However, the solution must be simple, require minimal learning curve, and demonstrate clear ROI given their time poverty and financial constraints.
