# Dispatcher Research Summary — February 2026

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes insights from dispatcher interviews and persona analysis conducted in February 2026. The research focuses on dispatchers working in small to medium-sized transportation companies (4-30 trucks), who represent the primary target segment for EW Office Navigation fleet licensing.

## Key Findings

### Core Role & Environment

Dispatchers operate in a **highly reactive, real-time environment** where they must keep transports moving without disruption. They work under constant pressure, switching between multiple tools and communication channels to manage drivers, handle incidents, and ensure complete documentation.

### Primary Goals

Dispatchers' primary goal is to **keep transports running smoothly and close trips quickly and accurately** with full documentation. They need to react immediately to delays, incidents, and changes on the road while ensuring all paperwork is complete for invoicing.

### Critical Pain Points

1. **Tool Fragmentation**: Dispatchers waste significant time switching between WhatsApp, portals, telematics systems, Excel spreadsheets, and document applications. This fragmentation causes errors, delays, and financial losses.

2. **Documentation Dependency**: Slow or incomplete photos/scans from drivers delay invoicing and cause rework. Dispatchers spend hours collecting, correcting, and manually settling accounts in Excel.

3. **Manual Workload**: Despite working in structured organizations (medium/large companies), dispatchers still export data to Excel for cost breakdowns and trip settlements, indicating a lack of integrated systems.

4. **Context Switching**: Constant switching between maps, calls, messages, portals, and spreadsheets creates cognitive overload and increases error rates.

### Key Differences by Company Size

**Small Company Dispatchers (0-10 trucks):**
- Highly reactive, constantly switching between multiple tools
- Real-time, high-pressure environment
- Manual + multi-tool workflow (Excel, telematics portals, chat apps, PDFs)
- Documentation-dependent — delays cause invoicing problems

**Medium/Large Company Dispatchers (11-50 trucks):**
- Structured but still reactive
- Need a single workspace to see: status, documents, costs, lifecycle state
- Require fast, smooth workflows (minimal clicks, no repeated credential checks)
- Still rely on manual Excel exports for cost breakdowns

### What Dispatchers Want

- **Single operational hub** — one place to see all transport information
- **Real-time alerts** — immediate notification of problems
- **Automatic cost/document capture** — reduce manual data entry
- **Fast, smooth workflows** — no clumsy navigation, minimal clicks
- **Timely documents from drivers** — support invoicing without delays

### Implications for Fleet Licensing

Dispatchers value **centralized management and automation**. They want tools that reduce manual work and provide visibility. A fleet licensing solution that integrates seamlessly into their workflow and reduces tool fragmentation would address core pain points. However, the solution must be fast, reliable, and require minimal learning curve given their time-poor environment.
