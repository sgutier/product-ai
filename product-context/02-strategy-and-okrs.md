# Strategy & OKRs — EW Navigation App

## Strategic role of EW Navigation App

EW Navigation App plays three strategic roles:

1. **Driver-facing execution layer**
   - Where transport plans become reality
   - Primary touchpoint during trip execution

2. **Adoption driver for EW Office**
   - Makes Office value tangible for drivers
   - Reduces friction between planning and execution

3. **Monetization surface**
   - Enables premium services, subscriptions, and bundles
   - Potential for both B2C and B2B monetization models

---

## Strategic themes (current)

- Consolidation of navigation experience
- Stronger EW Office integration
- Transition from pure freemium to **selective monetization**
- Quality and reliability as table stakes
- Segmentation of drivers and customers

---

## Typical OKR alignment (illustrative)

### Objective: Increase value captured from EW Navigation users
- KR: Increase share of drivers exposed to premium features
- KR: Improve conversion or attach rate to paid offerings
- KR: Grow Navigation-attributed revenue

---

### Objective: Improve transport execution experience
- KR: Increase route execution success rate
- KR: Reduce navigation-related support tickets
- KR: Improve driver satisfaction / app ratings

---

### Objective: Strengthen EW Office ecosystem
- KR: Increase % of Office transports executed via EW Navigation
- KR: Improve dispatcher–driver workflow completion

---

## Non-goals (explicit)

- Embedded / OEM navigation strategy
- Custom navigation solutions for specific OEMs
- Navigation engine R&D beyond product needs

These are handled elsewhere.
