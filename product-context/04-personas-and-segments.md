# Personas & Segments — EW Navigation App

This document defines the **canonical user segments and personas** for EW Navigation App.
All initiatives should explicitly state **which persona(s)** they target.

---

## Primary Segments

### 1. Company Driver (Employed Driver)

**Description**
- Driver employed by a transport company
- Uses navigation as part of daily work
- Often uses a company-provided tablet or phone

**Key jobs-to-be-done**
- Execute assigned routes accurately
- Avoid truck restrictions and fines
- Finish jobs on time with minimal friction

**Pain points**
- Mismatch between dispatcher-planned route and navigation
- Last-minute changes not reflected in navigation
- Low tolerance for instability or crashes

**What they value**
- Reliability and clarity
- “Just works” experience
- Minimal interaction during driving

**Relevance to EW Office**
- High — primary beneficiary of Office ↔ Navigation integration

---

### 2. Owner-Operator (Self-employed)

**Description**
- Owns and drives their own truck
- Makes both operational and business decisions
- Often price-sensitive

**Key jobs-to-be-done**
- Navigate efficiently and compliantly
- Reduce costs (fuel, fines, delays)
- Manage trips with minimal overhead

**Pain points**
- Fragmented tools
- Paying for features without clear ROI
- Complex or opaque pricing

**What they value**
- Tangible savings
- Clear value for money
- Control and transparency

**Relevance to EW Office**
- Medium to high — potential Office upsell via Navigation

---

### 3. Mixed / BYOD Driver

**Description**
- Driver uses own device but drives for a company
- Often outside strict IT or MDM control

**Key jobs-to-be-done**
- Execute company routes
- Use familiar tools on personal device

**Pain points**
- Login friction
- Account / entitlement confusion
- Inconsistent experience across devices

**What they value**
- Simple onboarding
- Clear access to assigned work

---

## Secondary Stakeholders (Indirect)

### Dispatcher / Planner (via EW Office)
- Plans routes and transport orders
- Expects 1:1 execution fidelity in Navigation
- Cares about predictability and feedback

---

## Segmentation Dimensions (used in initiatives)

Initiatives may segment users by:
- Employment type (company vs self-employed)
- Device ownership (company device vs BYOD)
- Geography / regulation
- Exposure to EW Office workflows
- Monetization eligibility

---

## Non-target users (explicit)

- Passenger car drivers
- Embedded / OEM navigation users
- Non-professional drivers

These are out of scope.
