# Pricing & Monetization — EW Navigation App

This document summarizes the **current and emerging monetization context** for EW Navigation App.
It is intended to inform initiative hypotheses and constraints.

---

## Current state (baseline)

- EW Navigation App is primarily **freemium**
- Large active user base
- Historically **low direct monetization**
- Value largely captured indirectly (ecosystem, stickiness)

---

## Legacy context (relevant)

- Sygic Truck historically used:
  - Lifetime licenses
  - Paid add-ons (maps, features)
- This context informs expectations of some users
- Not all legacy models are desirable going forward

---

## Strategic monetization directions (in scope)

### 1. Premium Navigation Features
- Paid access to advanced or professional features
- May include soft paywalls or subscriptions
- Requires careful segmentation and experimentation

---

### 2. EW Office–Driven Monetization
- Navigation as part of Office bundles
- Value realized through Office subscription uplift
- Navigation strengthens Office retention and adoption

---

### 3. Hybrid Models
- Freemium baseline + paid enhancements
- Different offers for:
  - Company drivers
  - Owner-operators
  - Office customers vs standalone users

---

## Key monetization principles

- Do not break core navigation trust
- Monetization must be:
  - Transparent
  - Predictable
  - Clearly valuable
- Avoid surprise paywalls during critical workflows
- Guard against store policy violations (Apple / Google)

---

## Known constraints

- App Store / Play Store policies
- Existing user expectations
- Legal and contractual dependencies (e.g. map providers)
- Support and operational readiness

---

## Open questions (typical)

- What users are willing to pay, and for what
- Optimal pricing model per segment
- How to align Navigation monetization with Office pricing
- How to experiment without damaging ratings or trust
